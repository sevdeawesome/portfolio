BACKGROUND

The Laser Interferometer Gravitational Wave Observatory (LIGO) is an organization that operates multiple 4km long michelson interferometers to detect gravitational waves. It is the most precise measuring tool ever created. In 2017, Dr. Barry Barish, a UCR faculty member, won the Nobel prize in physics for leading a team at LIGO to detect the first ever gravitational wave. Most LIGO research, like mine, is on finding ways to improve the detectors sensitivity to see more gravitational wave causing events (like black hole mergers or supernovae) further out in spacetime.
